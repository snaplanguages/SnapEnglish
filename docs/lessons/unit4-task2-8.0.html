QUESTION:
You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.

In the past, knowledge was contained in books. Nowadays, knowledge is uploaded to the Internet. Do the advantages of this outweigh the disadvantages?

Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience. Write at least 250 words.

(1) In the past, knowledge could only be found in books. (2) However, these days, everyone can get knowledge, with the help of the Internet. (3) This essay will present the advantages and disadvantages of online knowledge.

(4) On the one hand, uploading knowledge to the Internet is detrimental. (5) Firstly, the knowledge will be robbed. (6) The reason is that there are many illegal websites, such as Z-Library which give user the permission to download knowledge for free. (7) Secondly, learners will be overwhelmed as there are too much information and fake news on the Internet. (8) Therefore, they will not learn anything at all. (9) Finally, there are lots of fake news and false stories on the Internet. (10) As a result, learners will have wrong knowledge, develop distorted thinking and false believes. (11) In the future, these people will turn out to be close-minded people.

(12) On the other hand, despite the aforementioned disadvantages, I believe the benefits are more noteworthy. (13) Firstly, learners can download book course for free and conveniently. (14) To exemplify, I can easily download Harry Potter epub or PDF files of the books for free. (15) They also do not have to carry heavy books in their bags with them to school. (16) In contrast, they can easily open whatever knowledge they need with smartphone. (17) They can also share the knowledge with friends. (18) Secondly with e-books, publishers do not have to cut down trees to produce paper. (19) Therefore, they will reduce logging and save the environment.

(20) In conclusion, despite there are several disadvantages of this practice, I believe the benefits are still a lot stronger.

EXERCISE 2: SOFTEN THE TONES OF THE FOLLOWING SENTENCES

Hãy làm giảm nhẹ mức độ khẳng định (soften the tone) của các câu sau để phù hợp với văn phong học thuật.

1) (1) In the past, knowledge could only be found in books.
2) (7) Secondly, learners will be overwhelmed as there is too much information.
3) (9) Finally, there are lots of fake news and false stories on the Internet.
4) (18) Secondly, with e-books, publishers do not have to cut down trees to produce paper.
5) (19) Therefore, online knowledge will reduce logging and save the environment.
Gợi ý (Tham khảo):
1) In the past, knowledge was primarily confined to books. (Chủ yếu giới hạn trong sách, thay vì 'chỉ có thể').
2) Secondly, learners may feel / might be overwhelmed... (Sử dụng may/might thay vì will).
3) Finally, the Internet is rife with / there is a prevalence of fake news... (Thay vì nói 'there are lots of').
4) Secondly, with e-books, societies can reduce their reliance on paper production. (Giảm sự phụ thuộc, thay vì 'không phải chặt cây').
5) Therefore, this transition can contribute to environmental sustainability by reducing the need for logging.
Về tiêu chí Task Response (TR):
  • Một bài Band 6 thường thiếu một kết luận rõ ràng về việc tại sao Ưu điểm lại vượt trội hơn Nhược điểm (hoặc ngược lại).
  • Hãy luôn mở rộng ý tưởng để lập luận trở nên rõ ràng và được hỗ trợ tốt hơn.
  • Đừng nghĩ quá nhiều về việc tìm ý tưởng; thay vào đó, hãy nghĩ cách làm sao để phát triển những ý tưởng đó.
  • Thận trọng với ngôn ngữ tuyệt đối (strong language); hãy sử dụng ngôn ngữ linh hoạt và mang tính khả năng (softer language / hedging).

2. COHERENCE AND COHESION (Độ mạch lạc và Tính liên kết)

Bây giờ hãy cùng tìm hiểu lý do bài viết này nhận điểm 5-6 ở tiêu chí CC. Trích xuất mô tả Band 6:

  • "Information and ideas are generally arranged coherently and there is a clear overall progression." (Thông tin và ý tưởng nhìn chung được sắp xếp mạch lạc, có sự phát triển logic rõ ràng).

Học viên đã tổ chức các đoạn văn khá rõ ràng. Tuy nhiên, có sự lặp lại thông tin (information overlap) giữa các câu (7) và (9). Việc dùng "Secondly" ở câu (7) và "Finally" ở câu (9) khiến người đọc tưởng đây là hai ý khác nhau, nhưng thực chất cả hai câu đều đang nói về vấn đề "fake news" (tin giả).

Unclear progression (Mạch lạc kém) Clearer progression (Mạch lạc tốt hơn)

(9) Finally, there are lots of fake news and false stories on the Internet. (10) As a result, learners will have wrong knowledge, develop distorted thinking and false believes.   • "Cohesive devices are used to some good effect but cohesion within and/or between sentences may be faulty or mechanical due to misuse, overuse or omission." (Từ nối được sử dụng khá tốt nhưng có thể bị lỗi, mang tính máy móc do lạm dụng, dùng sai hoặc bỏ sót).

Học viên đã lạm dụng quá nhiều từ nối (connectors) và luôn đặt chúng ở đầu câu. Ví dụ đoạn 1: (4) On the one hand... (5) Firstly... (6) The reason is that... (7) Secondly... (8) Therefore... (9) Finally... (10) As a result...
Thực tế, nhiều câu có thể được gộp lại để tạo sự trôi chảy tự nhiên (flow) tốt hơn.

4. LEXICAL RESOURCE (Vốn từ vựng)

Về tiêu chí Từ vựng (LR):

  • Chú ý đến sắc thái của từ (tone), một số từ có thể mang nghĩa quá mạnh hoặc quá nhẹ không phù hợp với văn phong học thuật.

UPGRADING BAND 6 TO BAND 8

Bây giờ chúng ta đã hiểu rõ các vấn đề trong bài viết Band 6 của học viên, hãy cùng xem phiên bản nâng cấp lên Band 8 nhé.

In the past, knowledge was primarily confined to books. However, today's technological era has enabled everyone to acquire knowledge effortlessly. While this digital transition presents several drawbacks, I firmly believe they are outweighed by the advantages.

On the one hand, uploading knowledge to the Internet can be detrimental. Firstly, it exposes valuable content, such as e-books, to the risk of digital piracy, where unauthorized copies spread widely on pirate websites. This copyright infringement may cause substantial financial harm to publishers and authors, whose hard work might go uncompensated. Secondly, since the Internet is rife with fabricated news, distinguishing truth from falsehood can be a challenge for readers. Exposed to false information, those who lack critical thinking skills may adopt false beliefs, which might lead to misguided decisions.

Uploading knowledge on the Internet, I believe, offers substantial benefits that outweigh the drawbacks. Firstly, everyone can access information rapidly with a simple click, significantly reducing the time and effort required. Compared to traditional library research that could take hours or even days, online knowledge offers unparalleled efficiency and accessibility. Secondly, by switching to e-books and online storage, societies can reduce their reliance on paper production. This digital transition, therefore, can contribute to environmental sustainability by reducing the need for logging.

In conclusion, although online knowledge dissemination can negatively affect authors, publishers, and those lacking critical thinking, they are outweighed by the benefits for the general public and the environment. However, it is still important to implement solutions, such as firewalls, subscriptions, fact-checking, and fostering digital literacy, to effectively address the challenges.

EXERCISE 1: SELF-ASSESSMENT CHECKLIST (BẢNG KIỂM TRA TỰ CHẤM ĐIỂM)

In the world of education, many schools have integrated subjects such as mathematics and philosophy into the core school curriculum. This raises a crucial question - are the benefits of introducing these subjects greater than any possible drawbacks?

On the downside, these subjects can bring many disadvantages. Firstly, they are often demanding and time-consuming, and some students may feel stressful and overwhelmed. As a result, the time spent delving into these subjects might take time away from other important ones, such as vocational training. Furthermore, mathematics and philosophy might not help students find jobs when they graduate from school, so studying these subject may be considered a waste of time. Finally, student interests and learning styles are different, and they might not like the abstract nature of philosophy. Therefore, students may feel disengaged and disinterested.

However, I would contend that teaching mathematics and philosophy also offer important benefits. Firstly, mathematics allow students to think logically. Logical thinking skills can be applied to any careers. By the same token, philosophy makes students more tolerant, acceptable, and understanding towards people of different cultures. Therefore, students will have better personality development. Finally, delving into these subjects early on gives students a solid foundation for a broader things in life, allowing them to swim in our diverse and globalized world.

In conclusion, while teaching mathematics and philosophy may bring several disadvantages, it also brings substantial benefits. Therefore, we should carefully consider the best ways to balance these aspects and maximize the advantages.

EXERCISE 2: READ ANOTHER SAMPLE ANSWER (BAND 8) AND ANSWER THE QUESTIONS

People are still divided on the sets of subjects that are mandatory for every student. Mathematics and philosophy are among the subjects that people think should be made compulsory. In my opinion, although there are several disadvantages, they are outweighed by more significant benefits.

With mathematics, the curriculum in schools frequently places excessive emphasis on numerical formulas that are of little help to learners' day-to-day lives. Similarly, philosophy heavily delves into social theories and ideologies, often at a macro-level, which might not yield immediate practical advantages. Mathematics not only enhances numeracy skills, it also fosters logical thinking and quantitative reasoning, enhancing students' ability to analyze and make informed decisions. In other words, mathematics and philosophy can enrich students' intellectual growth, teaching them skills that can transcend career boundaries.

In conclusion, while there might be concerns about the practicality and relevance of teaching mathematics and philosophy, I firmly believe that the advantages, including enhanced logical-thinking skills, empathy and understanding far outweigh any potential drawbacks. After all, the aim of education is to cultivate well-rounded citizens rather than individuals with high earning potential.
